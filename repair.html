92 million tonnes of textile waste is produced globally every year*

Elongating the lifecycle of garments through repair is key to reducing this eye-wateringly large amount of textile waste.

The resources on this page target a wide range of basic repairs, using both articles and videos.

Holes

Often the result of wear and tear, clothing holes are the most common repair and manifest themselves in many ways. The type of repair required often depends on the location of the hole and your garments fabric.

How to hand sew a seam that has ripped

This guide takes you through an invisible seam repair. Starting by turning the garment inside out and lining up the seam that has ripped with pins, this technique can be applied to other small holes for an invisble mend. If hand-sewing isn't your thing or the hole is covering the length of a long seam, try using a sewing machine.

No-sew fix to repair a small hole

If sewing really isn't your thing, this guide teaches you how to fix small holes using fusing paper & and an iron!

How to repair a shirt collar

A quick and easy tutorial to reinforce and fix a collar.
